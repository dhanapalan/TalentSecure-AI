# Quantitative Aptitude — Percentages

## Core Concepts

A percentage is a fraction with denominator 100. To convert a fraction to a percentage, multiply by 100. To convert a percentage to a fraction, divide by 100.

- x% of y = (x × y) / 100
- x% of y is the same as y% of x
- If a value increases by r%, the new value = original × (1 + r/100)
- If a value decreases by r%, the new value = original × (1 − r/100)

## Successive Percentage Change

If a value changes by a% and then by b%, the net change is:
a + b + (a × b)/100 percent.

Example: A price increases by 20% and then decreases by 10%.
Net change = 20 − 10 + (20 × −10)/100 = 10 − 2 = 8% increase.

## Percentage Change Reversal

If A's salary is 25% more than B's, then B's salary is 20% less than A's — NOT 25% less.
Formula: if x% more, the reverse is (100x / (100 + x))% less.
If x% less, the reverse is (100x / (100 − x))% more.

## Population / Depreciation Problems

Population after n years with r% annual growth: P × (1 + r/100)^n
Machine value after n years with r% depreciation: P × (1 − r/100)^n

Example: A town of 10,000 grows 10% per year. After 2 years: 10,000 × 1.1 × 1.1 = 12,100.

## Common Fraction ↔ Percentage Equivalents

- 1/2 = 50%, 1/3 = 33.33%, 1/4 = 25%, 1/5 = 20%
- 1/6 = 16.67%, 1/8 = 12.5%, 1/10 = 10%, 1/12 = 8.33%
- 3/4 = 75%, 2/3 = 66.67%, 5/8 = 62.5%

Memorizing these speeds up TCS NQT and Infosys aptitude questions dramatically.

## Profit & Loss (Percentage Application)

- Profit% = (Profit / Cost Price) × 100 — always on cost price unless stated otherwise
- Selling Price = CP × (1 + Profit%/100)
- If two items sell at the same price, one at x% profit and the other at x% loss, the seller ALWAYS incurs a net loss of (x²/100)%.

Example: Two watches sold at ₹990 each, one at 10% profit, one at 10% loss.
Net loss = (10²/100)% = 1% of total cost.

## Exam Traps to Avoid

1. Percentage OF a number vs percentage CHANGE — read carefully.
2. Successive discounts of 20% and 30% are NOT 50% — they are 44%.
3. "Increased BY 200%" means 3× the original; "increased TO 200%" means 2×.
4. Base matters: a 50% increase followed by a 50% decrease leaves you at 75%, not 100%.
